Other study team members include:
Linfeng Li, School of Information, University of Michigan

Invitation to be Part of a Research Study

We invite you to be part of a research study about simple games and Wikipedia editing.
Taking part in this research project is voluntary.

Important Information about the Research Study

Things you should know:

  • The purpose of the study is to understand how to introduce students to Wikipedia editing.
  • If you choose to participate, you will be asked to finish one computerized experiment and one Wikipedia editing activity. The computerized experiment will take 45 minutes and the Wikipedia editing activity should take no more than 1 hour.
  • We do not foresee any risks or discomforts from this research
  • As mentioned in the Syllabus, this study might improve your understanding of Econ 401 concepts. You will earn bonus points for your participation.
  • Taking part in this research project is voluntary. You don't have to participate, and you can stop at any time.

Please take time to read this entire form before deciding whether to take part in this research project.

What is the study about and why are we doing it?

The purpose of the study is to understand how to introduce students to Wikipedia editing. The Wikipedia articles selected for the study are related to the content of Econ 401. Therefore, the activity is complementary to the class.

What will happen if you take part in this study?

If you agree to take part in this study, you will be asked to first complete a computerized experiment and then finish a Wikipedia editing activity. Both tasks are accessible from a computer with a browser, and available 24/7. You can decide when and where to participate in these tasks. You will receive bonus points for Econ 401 based on your performance in our study.

The computerized experiment consists of a series of simple games and a survey. This experiment will take approximately 45 minutes and will start right afer you click on the "Next" button at the bottom of this page. You will be able to participate in only one experimental session.

The Wikipedia editing activity will be announced through an email, shortly after your completion of the computerized experiment. The email contains links to Wikipedia articles selected for this study. Editing the assigned Wikipedia articles will enable you to earn bonus points for Econ 401. The amount of bonus points depends on the quality of your edit. You will need to register for a Wikipedia account so we can verify your editing activities.

For this study, we will not link your data to external data, except for what we will collect from the two activities. The identifiable information will include: first and last name from the computerized experiment, email account for the computerized experiment and your Wikipedia username.

How could you benefit from this study?

Although you will not directly benefit from being in this study, others might benefit because you will help us understand how to introduce students to Wikipedia editing. As the content of Wikipedia is publicly available, your edits contribute to the improvement of Wikipedia articles. As such, they also contribute to human knowledge in the public domain.

What risks might result from being in this study?

The experiment does not involve physical discomfort. The study poses no more than minimal risk. There is a possible risk of "breach of confidentiality" and the PIs shall mitigate such risks by ensuring the security standard of data storage formats, careful management of access to the data, and de­identification of data before any data re­use and redistribution.

How will we protect your information?

We plan to publish the results of this study. To protect your privacy, we will not include any information that could directly identify you.

We will protect the confidentiality of your research records by removing your personal identifiable information right after we received the complete responses from you. Your name and any other information that can directly identify you will be stored separately from the data collected as part of the project. Yet, by the nature of how Wikipedia is maintained, your Wikipedia username and your edits to the Wikipedia article will remain publicly available.

It is possible that other people may need to see the information we collect about you. These people work for the University of Michigan, and government offices that are responsible for making sure the research is done safely and properly.

What will happen to the information we collect about you after the study is over?

We will keep your research data to use for future research. Your name and other information that can directly identify you will be kept secure and stored separately from the research data collected as part of the project.

We may share your research data with other investigators without asking for your consent again, but it will not contain information that could directly identify you. If data will be deposited in a public or other repository, we will remove all personal identifiers, including your name and the Wikipedia username.

How will we compensate you for being part of the study?

You will earn extra credits from both activities in this study. In the computerized experiment, you will earn experiment tokens by playing several simple games and filling out one short survey. The conversion rate of experiment tokens to bonus points will be announced in the lecture. From the Wikipedia Editing task, you will also earn bonus points based on the quality of your edits.

Your Participation in this Study is Voluntary

It is totally up to you to decide to whether to participate in this research study. Participating in this study is voluntary. Even if you decide to be part of the study now, you may change your mind and stop at any time. You do not have to answer any questions you do not want to answer. If you decide to withdraw before this study is completed, we will keep a copy of your data. The study team will not terminate your participation.
